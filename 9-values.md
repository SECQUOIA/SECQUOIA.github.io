At SECQUOIA, how we do research matters as much as what we discover. These values describe how we actually try to work, what we hold each other to, and what we look for in the people who join us.

### How we work

## Integrity above everything else

Mistakes are part of research. What matters is that we acknowledge them, learn from them, and never cover them up. Unethical behavior and the refusal to admit error are the only true disqualifiers in this group, regardless of brilliance. Day to day, integrity looks like attributing ideas in conversation, naming uncertainty rather than projecting false confidence, and correcting yourself publicly when you were wrong.

## Mean it

We are here because we genuinely care about the ideas, the methods, and the people we work with. We believe hard things can be done, and we attempt them because they matter.

## Follow the question

The best research follows the question wherever it leads. We protect that freedom against anything that would narrow it prematurely: administrative friction, sunk-cost thinking, or pressure to produce expected results. External constraints shape what we can pursue; we accept that. Within them, we follow the question rather than the answer we were hoping for. Dead ends are part of the work, not signs that something went wrong.

## Open science

We publish our code, data, and methods because science builds on itself. Maintaining software others rely on is real work, and exposing your methods publicly means your mistakes are visible to everyone. We do it anyway.

## No shortcuts on quality

No deadline, reviewer, grant, or competitive pressure justifies sacrificing rigor or crossing ethical lines. We would rather submit late or keep working than put something into the world we are not confident in.

### How we think

## Intellectual humility

No language, solver, framework, or methodology deserves our allegiance beyond whether it fits the problem. We hold our ideas loosely enough to abandon them when the evidence says so. When someone challenges our thinking, we engage with the argument, not the person, with the same openness we ask of others. Dogmatic attachment to a paradigm, or to a result we want to be true, gets in the way of the work.

## Give credit

We name who did what: in papers, in presentations, in conversation. We credit collaborators, prior work, and the ideas we build on. This is how trust is built and how science compounds over time.

### How we relate

## Community at every level

It takes a community to thrive. The lab is where we support each other daily. The department becomes our home. The broader optimization, quantum computing, and chemical engineering communities are the colleagues whose work we build on and whose careers we care about.

## Pass knowledge forward

Academia has a [hidden curriculum](https://scatter.wordpress.com/2018/07/24/guest-post-grad-schools-hidden-curriculum/): unwritten rules that advantage those who already know them. We make SECQUOIA's version explicit in the [PhD Handbook](/handbook/). Writing things down is part of it, but so is making time to teach, to answer questions, and to share what you have learned. Senior members do this because it is part of belonging to a research community. Each generation should leave the path clearer than it found it.

## Support where you want to go

SECQUOIA trains people for many paths: faculty positions, national labs, industry, startups, policy, and more. We support whatever direction is right for each person. Our [alumni](/2-members.html#alumni) reflect that range. Your goals here are part of why you are doing the work.

## Take care of ourselves and each other

Rigorous, creative research requires whole people. Sleep, rest, and a life outside the lab are preconditions for doing good work, not rewards for it. We do not glorify overwork. The obligation runs both ways: we actively look out for one another. See [Lab Counterculture](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3334480.3381824) by Hammer, To & Cruz for the broader argument we draw on here.

### Who we are

## Inclusion is not separate — it is foundational

Who is in the room shapes what gets discovered. Building a group where people from all backgrounds can contribute their best work takes active, ongoing effort: in how we recruit, how we structure mentorship across cultural and personal differences, and how we notice and address the ways academic environments can exclude. See the [DEI and Outreach page](/8-diversity.html) for the full statement and ongoing initiatives.
